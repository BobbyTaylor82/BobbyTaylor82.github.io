About

I’m an Electrical Engineer who graduated from the University of Missouri S&T with a passion for exploring data. I previously worked for Callaway Nuclear Power Plant for eight years where I utilized a team approach to develop structured cause analysis methods to determine significant adverse trends. In this role, I developed the ability to solve various challenges ranging from reducing unanticipated compressor downtime and identifying integrity issues with the fire protection system using Excel and Wonderware eDNA software. Wonderware eDNA software is a real-time data management software which bridges the gaps between information technology and operations.

What lead me to change my career path ???

There is a saying, " The only thing constant in life is change." In December 2016, I decided to leave to take care of my grandfather. Although this was a difficult period in my life, I knew everything happens for a reason. Callaway was a great opportunity which exposed me to how companies use excel, SQL, and models to solve problems. After my grandfather passed away, I continued my search for engineering opportunities and moved to Houston.




My Route into Exploratory Data Analysis

While in Houston, I discovered an emerging field called, “Data Analytics.” Around this time, I met a guy named Asela in a Data Analytics BootCamp, I attended in Houston. He was far more experienced in the analytics domain than me. We would talk statistics and explore datasets using the Pandas library until the small hours of the morning. Next, I decided to take a few Udemy courses to explore this new field. The classes were great!!! I got to refresh my statistics and python skills, but also learn more about exploratory data libraries, such as pandas and various mapping libraries. Around this time, I also found out about kaggle and decided to explore a few datasets. I figured exploring these datasets would help hone my analytical thinking skills.
