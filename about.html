About

I’m an Electrical Engineer who graduated from the University of Missouri S&T with a passion for exploring data. I previously worked for Callaway Nuclear Power Plant for eight years where I utilized a team approach to develop structured cause analysis methods to determine significant adverse trends. In this role, I developed the ability to solve various challenges ranging from reducing unanticipated compressor downtime and identifying integrity issues with the fire protection system using Excel.

What lead me to change my career path ???

There is a saying, " The only thing constant in life is change." In December 2016, I was laid-off. Although this was a confusing period in my life, I knew everything happens for a reason. It was a great opportunity which exposed me to how companies use Excel, SQL, and Regression Models to solve problems. After applying for a few opportunities and moving to Houston, I decided to step back from the application process to really focus on a career path I would love to pursue.




My Route into Exploratory Data Analysis

After months of internet searching and talking to various people, I discovered an emerging field called, “Data Analytics.” Around this time, I met a guy named Asela in a Data Analytics BootCamp, I attended in Houston. He was far more experience in the analytics domain than me. We would talk statistics and explored datasets using the Pandas library until the small hours of the morning. Next, I decided to take a few Udemy courses to further explore this new field. The courses were great!!! I got to refresh my statistics and python skills, but also learn more about data exploratory libraries, such as pandas and various mapping libraries. Around this time, I also found out about kaggle and decided to explore a few datasets. I figured exploring these datasets would help hone my statistical thinking skills.
