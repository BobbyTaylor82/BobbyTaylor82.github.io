Greater St.Louis Area

Posted 4/19/2018 by Bobby Taylor


As I traveled from the home of Willie Nelson back to the birthplace of Chuck Berry, I started reflecting on the things I've experienced and the people I've encountered. I can tell you leaving St. Louis was a hard decision but necessary. For me, I needed to experience other cities.


Now, I am back in St.louis wondering what should be my next move. So, I decided to explore a dataset I found rambling through the interwebs. In this quick EDA, I used an interactive mapping library, JavaScript, HTML, CSS, and pandas.The records in this dataset were pretty straightforward and no data cleaning was needed. Each record contained eleven features. The features were the following:


Feature Names

  • Municipality
  • Population
  • Sales Tax
  • Fines and Fees
  • FF Per Capita
  • ST per Capita
  • General Revenue
  • FF as Percent of General Revenue
  • Without Sales Tax
  • With Fines and Fees under Cap
  • Best Course

So first, I decided to map the top 10 populated areas in St.Louis and I wanted to know the revenue for each municipality. I was amazed that Florissant had the highest population and University City generated the most revenue.





Next, I decided to group by municipality to visualize the sale tax and fines & fees per capita and the results were startling. It appears Ferguson has the highest sale tax along with fines & fees per capita but generates the lowest revenue and has the smallest population.





Findings:

The goal of this EDA was to practice interactive mapping with a real dataset and learn more about front-end developement. In the process, I discovered a few findings which pose even deeper questions which are outside of my domain. Some of the questions are the following:

  • Why does Ferguson and Maryland Heights have the highest fine & fees per capita and how can St.louis solve this problem?
  • The population size is inversely proportional to the general revenue in Wildwood. Maybe, there needs to be more businesses in this municipality.
  • What are some of the contributing factors in sales tax per capita and how can these values be lowered for all municipalities?
  • How can the general revenue be increased in Wildwood and Ferguson?

WHY...WHY..Create a blog???

Posted 4/18/2018 by Bobby Taylor

The goal of this blog is to visually explore different datasets and provide a summary of my findings. I chose to create this blog for a couple of different reasons.

  • If the old saying, "practice makes perfect" is true, then this will be an excellent medium to practice EDA. Often, I find myself only wanting to know a few different questions when exploring various datasets with nowhere to post my findings. So, I figured creating this blog would be a great place to showcase my results and learn a lot along the way.
  • A good way to practice front-end web development, web applications, and use different interactive mapping libraries.

This blog is pretty standard at this time, but as my skills grow, I will further design the front-end.
